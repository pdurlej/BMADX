# Plain Codex vs BMADX

This transcript is intentionally simplified. It is meant to show user-visible
difference in workflow shape, not to act as a lab-grade benchmark.

## Task

Add a pricing section to the landing page and keep the change small.

## Plain Codex shape

User:
> Add a pricing section to the landing page and keep the change small.

Typical first response:
- asks a few broad clarification questions
- may suggest multiple possible approaches
- may leave the process weight implicit

Reading:
- low setup
- but the user still has to steer scope and verification more manually

## BMADX shape

User:
> Use BMADX for this repo. Pick the lightest safe mode. Keep it lightweight unless BMAD is truly needed.
>
> My task:
> Add a pricing section to the landing page and keep the change small.
>
> What I care about:
> speed, clarity, and not overengineering it

Typical first response:
- chooses a bounded mode
- gives a short why
- gives a short next step
- keeps verification compact

Reading:
- more guidance than plain Codex
- less process burden than full BMAD

## Why this matters

This is the strongest day-to-day BMADX story:
- less manual process choice than plain Codex
- less weight than going full BMAD immediately
